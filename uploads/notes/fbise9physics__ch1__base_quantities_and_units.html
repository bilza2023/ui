Base Quantities & Base Units (7 Foundation Units)

Base (fundamental) quantities are measured independently; they are the backbone of the SI (Système International) on which all derived quantities are built.

The 7 SI Base Quantities

# Quantity Quantity Symbol SI Base Unit (name) Unit Symbol Dimension How we measure it (school-lab)
1 Length l metre m [L] Ruler, metre rod, vernier caliper
2 Mass m kilogram kg [M] Balance (digital or beam)
3 Time t second s [T] Stopwatch, clock
4 Electric current I ampere A [I] Ammeter in series
5 Thermodynamic temperature T kelvin K [Θ] Thermometer (°C) then convert to K
6 Amount of substance n mole mol [N] From mass & molar mass: n = m/M
7 Luminous intensity Iv candela cd [J] Light meter / photometer

Why “Base” Matters

  • Every derived unit is a product or ratio of these base units (e.g., newton = kg·m·s⁻²).
  • Dimensions track “what is being measured” regardless of unit system (helpful in checking formulas).

Mini Examples (Base vs Derived)

  1. Speed = distance / time → derived (m·s⁻1).
  2. Force = mass × acceleration → derived (kg·m·s⁻2).
  3. Temperature itself → base (K). Heat is energy → derived (J).
  4. Electric charge = current × time → derived (C = A·s).

Quick Operational Notes

  • Kelvin vs Celsius: K is the base unit. Convert using T(K) = θ(°C) + 273.15.
  • Mass vs Weight: Mass (kg) is base; weight is a force (N = kg·m·s⁻2) → derived.
  • Unit symbols never pluralize and take no full stop (use 5 kg, not 5 kgs.).

(FYI) Modern SI Reference Definitions (short)

Not examinable detail at Grade 9 typically, but good context:

  • second (s): 9,192,631,770 periods of radiation of 133Cs.
  • metre (m): distance light travels in 1/299,792,458 s (speed of light fixed).
  • kilogram (kg): defined by fixing Planck’s constant h; realized with a Kibble balance.
  • ampere (A): defined via fixed elementary charge e.
  • kelvin (K): defined via fixed Boltzmann constant kB.
  • mole (mol): contains exactly 6.02214076×1023 entities.
  • candela (cd): defined for light at 540×1012 Hz with 1/683 W·sr⁻1.
Core idea: Learn the seven base quantities, their unit symbols, and dimensions — everything else in physics is built from them.

Theorem 13.1.1 – In a Triangle, the Longer Side Has the Greater Opposite Angle

Book Statement

If two sides of a triangle are unequal in length, the longer side has an angle of greater measure opposite to it.

Key Vocabulary

  • Exterior Angle: An angle formed by one side of a triangle and the extension of an adjacent side.
  • Isosceles Triangle: A triangle with two equal sides and two equal base angles.

Construction

  1. In △ABC, take a point D on side AC such that AD = AB.
  2. Join BD; thus △ADB is isosceles.
  3. Label base angles: ∠ABD = ∠BDA (call them ∠1 and ∠2).

Proof

AD = ABConstruction
∠1 = ∠2Base angles of isosceles △ADB
∠ACB < ∠2Exterior-angle inequality in △BDC
∠ABC = ∠1 > ∠2∠1 = base angle at B
∠ABC > ∠ACBTransitive (∠1 > ∠2 > ∠ACB)

Conclusion

In △ABC, side AC is longer than AB; we have proved the angle opposite AC (namely ∠ABC) is greater than the angle opposite AB (namely ∠ACB). Hence, the longer side indeed subtends the larger angle.

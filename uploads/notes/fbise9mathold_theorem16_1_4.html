Theorem 16.1.4 – Triangles with Equal Altitudes Have Areas Proportional to Their Bases

Book Statement

Triangles with equal altitudes have areas proportional to their corresponding bases.

Key Vocabulary

  • Altitude (height): The perpendicular drawn from a vertex to the line containing the base.
  • Base: The side of the triangle to which the altitude is drawn.
  • Area ratio: When heights are equal, the ratio of areas equals the ratio of bases.

Symbolically, if two triangles have the same altitude h and respective bases b₁ and b₂, then [△₁]/[△₂] = b₁/b₂.

What We Will Prove

  1. Both triangles share the same perpendicular height to their bases.
  2. Using the area formula \(\tfrac{1}{2}\times \text{base} \times \text{height}\), the ratio of areas equals the ratio of bases.

Detailed Proof

Step 1 – Common Height

Let the two triangles have bases of lengths b₁ and b₂ and share the same perpendicular distance (altitude) h to those bases.

Step 2 – Express the Areas

Their areas are [△₁] = \(\tfrac{1}{2}\)·b₁·h and [△₂] = \(\tfrac{1}{2}\)·b₂·h.

Step 3 – Compare the Areas

Therefore, \([△₁]/[△₂] = (\tfrac{1}{2} b₁ h)/(\tfrac{1}{2} b₂ h) = b₁/b₂\), proving that triangles with equal altitudes have areas proportional to their bases.

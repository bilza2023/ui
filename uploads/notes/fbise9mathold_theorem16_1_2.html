Theorem 16.1.2 – Parallelograms on the Same Base and Between the Same Parallels Have Equal Areas

Book Statement

Parallelograms on the same base and between the same parallels are equal in area.

Key Vocabulary

  • Parallelogram: A quadrilateral with both pairs of opposite sides parallel.
  • Same base: The two figures share a common side used as the base.
  • Between the same parallels: Their opposite sides lie on two fixed parallel lines, so the perpendicular distance (height) is common.
  • Area of a parallelogram: Given by base × height.

If parallelograms ABCD and ABFE share base AB and the sides CD and FE lie on the same line parallel to AB, then [ABCD] = [ABFE].

What We Will Prove

  1. The perpendicular distance (height) from the line through CD and FE to the base line AB is the same for both parallelograms.
  2. Using the area formula (base × height) with equal base and equal height gives equal areas.

Detailed Proof

Step 1 – Common Height from Parallels

Let ABCD and ABFE be parallelograms on the same base AB, with CD and FE lying on a line ℓ parallel to AB. The perpendicular distance from ℓ to AB is constant; call it h. Thus, both parallelograms have the same height h relative to base AB.

Step 2 – Write the Areas

[ABCD] = AB × h and [ABFE] = AB × h, since both share base AB and height h.

Step 3 – Conclude Equality

Therefore, [ABCD] = [ABFE], proving that parallelograms on the same base and between the same parallels are equal in area.

Quick Snapshot

  • National AI Policy 2025 approved with a six‑pillar framework and a dedicated National AI Fund (via Ignite’s R&D pool). :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}
  • Public R&D capacity anchored by the National Center of Artificial Intelligence (NCAI) and its applied labs, accelerators, and training. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
  • Startup & skills pipeline supported by Ignite (NIC network, grants, large‑scale upskilling like DigiSkills) and AI‑focused events. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • Export momentum: ICT/IT exports have hit fresh highs in FY25, with sector commentary pointing to AI/ITeS as a growth lever. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Policy & Public Sector

Pakistan’s National Artificial Intelligence Policy 2025 lays out pillars for innovation, awareness, secure systems, sectoral adoption, infrastructure, and global partnerships, and earmarks a National AI Fund by allocating a portion of the existing R&D Fund managed by Ignite. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Ecosystem Drivers

  • NCAI (consortium model across universities) focuses on “hard” local problems, commercialization, and workforce development. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
  • Ignite runs NIC incubators, tech grants, and large‑scale skill programs (millions trained), with more AI‑centric showcases/competitions emerging. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Market Signals

  • IT/ITeS exports reported at ~US$3.8B in FY 2024‑25 (various reports), with policy targets pushing higher by 2029. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
  • Enterprise AI services continue to expand via local/global players with Pakistan engineering hubs (e.g., computer‑vision/ML solution firms). :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Challenges & What to Watch

  • Talent depth & retention: sustained advanced training and incentives.
  • Compute & data access: affordable GPUs, cloud credits, and shared datasets.
  • Responsible AI: translating policy into enforceable governance and sector playbooks. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
Core idea: With policy now in place, Pakistan’s AI momentum depends on execution—getting talent, compute, and commercialization to move in sync.

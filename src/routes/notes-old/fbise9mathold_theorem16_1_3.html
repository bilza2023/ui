Theorem 16.1.3 – A Triangle Has Half the Area of a Parallelogram on the Same Base and Between the Same Parallels

Book Statement

A triangle and a parallelogram on the same base and between the same parallels have areas in the ratio 1 : 2; that is, the area of the triangle is half the area of the parallelogram.

Key Vocabulary

  • Same base: The triangle and parallelogram share a common side as the base.
  • Between the same parallels: The base lies on one line and the opposite vertex/side lies on another line parallel to the base, giving a common perpendicular distance (height).
  • Area formulas: Triangle: \(\tfrac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}\); Parallelogram: \(\text{base} \times \text{height}\).

Symbolically, if triangle ABC and parallelogram ABDE share base AB and lie between the same parallels, then [△ABC] = \(\tfrac{1}{2}\)[ABDE].

What We Will Prove

  1. The perpendicular height to the common base is the same for both figures (due to parallels).
  2. Using the standard area formulas with equal base and equal height gives the 1 : 2 area ratio.

Detailed Proof

Step 1 – Common Height

Let triangle ABC and parallelogram ABDE have the same base AB and lie between parallels AB and CE. The perpendicular distance between these parallels is constant, so both figures share the same height h relative to base AB.

Step 2 – Express the Areas

The area of the triangle is [△ABC] = \(\tfrac{1}{2}\)·AB·h. The area of the parallelogram is [ABDE] = AB·h.

Step 3 – Compare

Therefore, [△ABC] : [ABDE] = (\tfrac{1}{2}·AB·h) : (AB·h) = 1 : 2, or equivalently [△ABC] = \(\tfrac{1}{2}\)[ABDE].

What is Physics?

Book Definition

Physics is the branch of science concerned with the nature and properties of matter and energy. It deals with concepts such as motion, force, energy, mass, and the behavior of the physical universe.

Key Vocabulary

  • Matter: Anything that has mass and occupies space.
  • Energy: The capacity to do work.
  • Physical Quantities: Measurable properties such as length, mass, and time.

Scope of Physics

Physics explains phenomena from the smallest particles to the largest galaxies. It provides the foundation for engineering, technology, and many other sciences.

Examples from Daily Life

  • The falling of an apple (gravity).
  • Boiling water (heat and thermodynamics).
  • Electric lights (electricity and electromagnetism).

Why Physics is Important

Physics helps us understand how the world works, predict natural events, and develop technologies that improve our lives.

SI Prefixes (milli → giga) & Symbols

SI prefixes scale units by powers of ten. They are written before the unit symbol (e.g., km, ms) and never stand alone.

Rules & Best Practices

  • Case matters: M (mega) vs m (milli); G (giga) vs g (gram).
  • Space between number and unit: 25 km, 3.0 ms.
  • No plurals / no full stop on symbols: 5 kg, not 5 kgs.
  • Mass quirk: SI base is kg, but prefixes attach to g: 1 mg = 10−3 g = 10−6 kg, 1 Mg = 106 g = 103 kg.
  • When converting areas/volumes, square/cube the whole factor (e.g., 1 cm² = 10−4 m²).

Fast Conversions (milli ↔ kilo ↔ mega ↔ giga)

  1. Time: 50 ms → s = 50 × 10−3 = 0.050 s
  2. Length: 7.2 km → m = 7.2 × 103 = 7200 m
  3. Energy: 3.5 MJ → J = 3.5 × 106 = 3,500,000 J
  4. Power: 0.12 GW → W = 0.12 × 109 = 1.2 × 108 W
  5. Mass: 450 mg → kg = 450 × 10−6 = 4.5 × 10−4 kg

Mini Practice (with answers)

  1. Convert: 2.4 × 103 m to km → Answer: 2.4 km
  2. Convert: 600 ms to s → Answer: 0.600 s
  3. Convert: 0.75 GJ to MJ → Answer: 750 MJ
  4. Convert: 3.2 hm to m → Answer: 320 m
Core idea: Move the decimal by the prefix power. From milli to kilo is 106 steps (−3 → +3); from kilo to giga is 106 (103 → 109).

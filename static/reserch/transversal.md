```md
A transversal is a line that cuts across two other lines.
If the other lines are parallel, the transversal creates special angle patterns.

* Look for Z, F, and U shapes — they reveal important rules.
```

```md
slide: Alternate Angles 🎞️ imageLeftBulletsRight 🎭 Tone: 🧩 Pattern Recognition ⏱️ EndTime: 25

Alternate angles appear **on opposite sides** of the transversal:

* Alternate Interior — inside the parallel lines
* Alternate Exterior — outside the parallel lines
* Both are always equal (congruent)

🖼️ *Image Prompt:* Highlight purple and blue angle pairs
🖼️ *Filename:* angles.jpg
```

---

```md
slide: Corresponding Angles 🎞️ imageLeftBulletsRight 🎭 Tone: 📐 Rule Learning ⏱️ EndTime: 20

Corresponding angles sit in the **same position** on both crossings.

* Think of “top-left with top-left”, “bottom-right with bottom-right”
* These angles are also equal (congruent)

🖼️ *Image Prompt:* Highlight yellow corresponding angles
🖼️ *Filename:* angles.jpg
```

---

```md
slide: Co-Interior Angles 🎞️ imageLeftBulletsRight 🎭 Tone: 🧮 Logic ⏱️ EndTime: 20

Same-side interior angles appear **on the same side** of the transversal, inside the parallels.

* These do not match — they add to 180° (supplementary)
```

# Postulate Explanation

## 1. SAS Postulate (Side-Angle-Side)

**📖 Book Reference:** Page 188  
**Used In:** Theorem 10.1.1

### 🔹 Statement:
If two sides and the included angle of one triangle are equal to two sides and the included angle of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent.

### 🔹 Notes:
This is a postulate — a rule accepted without proof. It forms the foundation for proving Theorem 10.1.1.

---

## 2. ASA Postulate (Angle-Side-Angle)

**📖 Book Reference:** Page 189  
**Used In:** Theorem 10.1.2

### 🔹 Statement:
If two angles and the included side of one triangle are equal to the corresponding parts of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent.

### 🔹 Notes:
Used inside the proof of Theorem 10.1.2. The theorem itself says: if two angles of a triangle are equal, then the sides opposite to those angles are also equal.

---

## 3. AAS Theorem (Angle-Angle-Side)

**📖 Book Reference:** Page 190  
**Used In:** Theorem 10.1.3

### 🔹 Statement:
If two angles and a non-included side of one triangle are equal to the corresponding two angles and side of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent.

### 🔹 Notes:
This is labeled as a theorem in the book, not a postulate — but it functions similarly in proving triangle congruence when ASA doesn’t apply.

---

## 4. CPCTC – Corresponding Parts of Congruent Triangles are Congruent

**📖 Book Reference:** Page 191  
**Used In:** Theorem 10.1.4

### 🔹 Statement:
If two triangles are congruent, then their corresponding sides and angles are equal.

### 🔹 Notes:
This is a derived result — used after proving congruence — and helps extend equality from the whole triangle to its parts.

Derived Quantities & Derived Units

Derived quantities are formed by combining base quantities through multiplication/division in formulas. Their SI derived units are built from base units (m, kg, s, A, K, mol, cd).

How to Recognize a Derived Quantity

  • Look for a formula linking two or more base quantities (e.g., density = mass / volume).
  • Write the unit in base units (e.g., N = kg·m·s⁻2).
  • Optionally write the dimension (e.g., Force: [M L T⁻²]).

Common Derived Quantities (Grade-9 Focus)

# Quantity Formula SI Unit (name, symbol) In Base Units Dimension Quick Context
1 Area A = l×w square metre, m² m² [L²] Surface of a desk, floor, page
2 Volume V = l×w×h cubic metre, m³ m³ [L³] Capacity of a box/bottle
3 Speed/Velocity v = s/t m s⁻¹ m·s⁻¹ [L T⁻¹] Car speed, wave speed
4 Acceleration a = Δv/Δt m s⁻² m·s⁻² [L T⁻²] Change of speed per second
5 Density ρ = m/V kg m⁻³ kg·m⁻³ [M L⁻³] Heaviness per volume (water ≈ 1000 kg m⁻³)
6 Force (Weight is a force) F = m a newton, N kg·m·s⁻² [M L T⁻²] Push/pull; weight = m·g
7 Work / Energy W = F s joule, J kg·m²·s⁻² [M L² T⁻²] Energy used when force moves an object
8 Power P = W/t watt, W kg·m²·s⁻³ [M L² T⁻³] Rate of doing work (bulbs, motors)
9 Pressure p = F/A pascal, Pa kg·m⁻¹· s⁻² [M L⁻¹ T⁻²] Force on each square metre (tyres, fluids)
10 Momentum p = m v kg m s⁻¹ kg·m·s⁻¹ [M L T⁻¹] “Quantity of motion” (collisions)
11 Charge Q = I t coulomb, C A·s [I T] Electricity passing in time
12 Potential difference V = W/Q volt, V kg·m²·s⁻³·A⁻¹ [M L² T⁻³ I⁻¹] Energy per charge (battery “push”)
13 Resistance R = V/I ohm, Ω kg·m²·s⁻³·A⁻² [M L² T⁻³ I⁻²] Opposition to current (wires, resistors)
14 Frequency cycles per second hertz, Hz s⁻¹ [T⁻¹] Oscillations per second (sound, waves)

Unit Building — Fast Derivations

  • N (newton) = kg·m·s⁻2 (from F = m a).
  • J (joule) = N·m = kg·m²·s⁻2.
  • W (watt) = J·s⁻1 = kg·m²·s⁻3.
  • Pa (pascal) = N·m⁻2 = kg·m⁻1·s⁻2.
  • V (volt) = J·C⁻1 = kg·m²·s⁻3·A⁻1.
  • Ω (ohm) = V·A⁻1 = kg·m²·s⁻3·A⁻2.

Mini Examples (Exam-Style)

  1. Show energy is derived: W = F s → (kg·m·s⁻2)·m = kg·m²·s⁻2.
  2. Find base units of pressure: p = F/A → (kg·m·s⁻2)/m² = kg·m⁻1·s⁻2.
  3. Momentum unit: p = m v → kg·(m·s⁻1) = kg·m·s⁻1.

Common Mistakes

  • Mixing unit systems (e.g., km with min) — convert to SI before calculating.
  • For area/volume conversions, square/cube the whole conversion factor (1 cm² = 10⁻4 m²).
  • Writing unit symbols with plurals or full stops (5 kg, not 5 kgs.).
Core idea: Start from the formula → substitute base units → simplify exponents. If the units match on both sides, your formula is consistent.

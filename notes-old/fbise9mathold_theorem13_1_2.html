Theorem 13.1.2 – In a Triangle, the Side Opposite the Greater Angle Is the Longer Side

Book Statement

If two angles of a triangle are unequal, the side opposite the greater angle is longer than the side opposite the smaller angle.

Key Vocabulary

  • Isosceles Triangle: A triangle with two equal sides and two equal base angles.
  • Exterior-Angle Inequality: An exterior angle of a triangle is greater than either opposite interior angle.

Construction

  1. In △ABC, suppose ∠ABC > ∠ACB. We must prove AC > AB.
  2. On side AC, mark point D so that AD = AB.
  3. Join BD; thus △ADB is isosceles with base angles ∠ABD = ∠BDA.

Conclusion

The side opposite the greater angle (here, AC opposite ∠ABC) is indeed longer than the side opposite the smaller angle (AB opposite ∠ACB). Hence, the converse of Theorem 13.1.1 is established.

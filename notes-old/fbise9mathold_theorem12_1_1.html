Theorem 12.1.1 – Any Point on the Perpendicular Bisector of a Line Segment Is Equidistant from Its End Points

Book Statement

Any point on the perpendicular bisector of a line segment is equidistant from its end points.

Key Vocabulary

  • Perpendicular Bisector: A line through the midpoint of a segment and perpendicular to it.
  • Equidistant: At the same distance from two or more points.

A perpendicular bisector behaves like a balance beam—every point on it is “centered” between the segment’s ends.

What We Will Prove

  1. Triangles △PCA and △PCB are congruent by SAS; hence PA = PB.

Detailed Proof

Let AB be a segment with midpoint C. The line LM through C, perpendicular to AB, is its perpendicular bisector. Pick any point P on LM and connect PA and PB.

In triangles △PCA and △PCB:

  • AC = CB (midpoint).
  • ∠PCA = ∠PCB = 90° (line LM is perpendicular to AB).
  • PC is common.

By the Side–Angle–Side (SAS) criterion, the triangles are congruent. Therefore, corresponding sides PA and PB are equal (CPCTC).

Conclusion

Any point on the perpendicular bisector of AB is equidistant from A and B.

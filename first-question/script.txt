

# 🧭 Anchor Script — "What is Algebra?"

This script matches the slide deck exactly: one title, ten EQ steps.  
Each section includes a short teaching anchor — not full speech, not too brief — just enough to guide natural narration.

---

## 🎬 Title Slide

**What is Algebra?**  
→ Introduce that this lesson begins the journey into algebra — the language of unknowns and patterns.

---

## 🧮 Step 1: Known vs Unknown

**Main idea:** Arithmetic uses known numbers; algebra allows unknowns.  
→ Help students contrast: “5 + 3” vs “x + 3”.  
→ Emphasize that algebra is not harder — it’s just more general.

---

## 🔤 Step 2: What is a Variable?

**Main idea:** A variable is a symbol (like x) for a number we don’t know yet.  
→ Say it’s just a placeholder — not mysterious.  
→ We use it when the value can change or is not yet known.

---

## ❓ Step 3: x, y, z Are Just Numbers

**Main idea:** Letters like x, y, z simply represent unknown numbers.  
→ Reassure them: these are not special — just names for missing values.  
→ Give a few quick examples: “x = 5”, “y = 10”.

---

## 🧾 Step 4: Algebraic Expressions

**Main idea:** We write things like x + 2, or 3x – 7, to represent patterns.  
→ These are called algebraic expressions.  
→ They are made from variables, numbers, and operations.

---

## 🧩 Step 5: Solving for x

**Main idea:** Algebra allows us to solve for the unknown.  
→ Example: “x + 2 = 5” → we can figure out x = 3.  
→ Solving means finding the number that makes both sides equal.

---

## 🔁 Step 6: Patterns and Rules

**Main idea:** Algebra helps simplify repeated patterns.  
→ For example: x + x + x = 3x  
→ It gives us shortcuts and structure.

---

## ⚖️ Step 7: Equations Show Balance

**Main idea:** Equations are like balance scales — both sides must be equal.  
→ “x + 4 = 10” means something + 4 gives 10.  
→ We solve to restore balance.

---

## 🌐 Step 8: General Rules for Any Number

**Main idea:** Algebra creates formulas that work for all numbers.  
→ Like: “a + b = b + a” — true for all values.  
→ That’s why we call it general or symbolic math.

---

## 🛠️ Step 9: Algebra in Real Life

**Main idea:** Algebra is used everywhere — science, business, logic.  
→ Not just classroom math — it powers formulas, software, and physics.  
→ It’s a universal tool, not just a subject.

---

## 🧠 Step 10: Why Learn Algebra?

**Main idea:** Learning algebra improves how you think.  
→ It trains your mind to find patterns, think clearly, and solve problems.  
→ It’s not just about math — it’s about mental skill.
